The 12 Most Famous Musical Pieces In History


1- Symphony 9 (Ludwig van Beethoven)

It was the most remarkable symphony of the time, not only because of its length but also for its final movement. It was the last symphony of Beethoven, which represented an important bridge between classical and romantic periods in the history of western music.


Play
2- The introduction of Falkyrie Opera (Richard Wagner)

It is the introduction of Richard Wagner's "Der Ring des Nibelungen" which is three minutes long and often heard alone. It is considered as one of Wagner's most famous pieces.


Play

3- The Four Seasons (Antonio Vivaldi)

Or Italian (Le Quatrro Stagioni), a collection composed by Italian composer Antonio Vivaldi for four violin instruments. Each one gives a musical expression of one of the seasons. It was written around 1720 and published in 1725 in Amsterdam.


Play
4- The Blue Danube Dance (Richard Strauss)

Or the German so-called (An Der Schönen Blauen Donau), it considered as one of the most famous pieces of waltz written by the Austrian composer Johann Strauss son in 1867. It summarizes the rich work of melodies and the diversity of the dance music of Strauss, which gave him the title (The King of the Waltz).

Play

5- Mass Of Death (Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart)

Mozart had begun to compose it in 1791 but he died at the same year before completing it.






Play
6- Mariage d'amour (Paul de Senneville)

Mariage d'amour: (English: Marriage of Love) is a piece of French solo piano music, composed by Paul de Senneville in 1979.
This version is sometimes erroneously attributed to (Frédéric Chopin) as (Spring Waltz) because of an upload on YouTube with the wrong title, which reached over 34 million views before being removed.


Play

7- Swan Lake (Pyotr Ilyich Tchaikovsky)

Ballet piece by Tchaikovsky composed in 1875-1876. Despite its initial failure, it is now one of the most popular Ballet pieces.The Bolshoi Ballet group performed it for the first time on the 4th of March 1877 at the Bolshoi Theater in Moscow.

Play
8- Tokata Vogue (Johann Christian Bach)

A two parts piece composed for the organ, it is believed that Bach had composed it before 1708. It is characterized by its majestic and dramatic melody, and its powerful rhythm. It was used at the beginning of "fantasy" the Disney's 1940 classic film.

Play

9- Hungarian Dances (Johannes Brahms)

A collection of 21 pieces composed by Johann Brahms, it was first written to be played just by pianists, and published in 1869-1880. Some of the pieces were arranged by Brahms himself, while others were arranged by his colleagues, including Antonin Dvorak.


Play
10- Short Night Music-Serenade 13 (Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart)

Serenade includes violin, viola, cello and double bass, composed by Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart, characterized by its lively cheerfulness and unforgettable tunes. The piece was completed on 10 August 1787 but was published after Mozart's death.

Play

11- The Fifth Symphony (Gustav Mahler)

Performed on 18 October 1904 in Cologne, the work was influenced by the composer's marriage in 1902 to Alma Schindler.Adagietto was his most famous music and is often used in romantic film scenes.


Play
12- Pavane (Gabriel Fauré)

It is composed by the French composer Gabriele Fourier in 1887. It is originally written as a piece for piano, but later was best known in the "Faure" orchestra. It acquired its slow rhythm from the Spanish court dance that has the same title.
