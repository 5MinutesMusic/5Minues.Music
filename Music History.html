The 12 Most Famous Musical Pieces In History


1- Symphony NO.9 (Ludwig Van Beethoven)

It was the most remarkable symphony of the time, not only because of its length but also for its final movement. It was the last symphony of Beethoven, which represented an important bridge between classical and romantic periods in the history of western music.

3- The Four Seasons (Antonio Vivaldi)

Or Italian (Le Quatrro Stagioni), a collection composed by Italian composer Antonio Vivaldi for four violin instruments. Each one gives a musical expression of one of the seasons. It was written around 1720 and published in 1725 in Amsterdam.

5- Mass Of Death (Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart)

The Mass of Death, a piece composed by Mozart in the year 1791 in Vienna before his death, and he did not complete it all. He completed it by his student Franz Javier Saussmayer and sent it to Count Franz von Fazlig whose wife died and when he played it in the Mass of the death of his wife, people knew that it was composed by Mozart.

Mariage d'amour: (English: Marriage of Love) is a piece of French solo piano music, composed by Paul de Senneville in 1979. Serenade includes violin, viola, cello and double bass, composed by Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart, characterized by its lively cheerfulness and unforgettable tunes. The piece was completed on 10 August 1787 but was published after Mozart's death.
